# qwen3.5:9b benchmark + optimisation (2026-06-01)

Model `qwen3.5:9b` (qwen35 arch, 9.7B, Q4_K_M) was pulled into `homeai-ollama`
~2h before this eval, replacing `qwen2.5:7b` and `phi4:14b` (both **deleted** —
configs still reference them, so the hot tier currently 404s).

Benchmarked via `services/model-evaluator/run_benchmark.py` (baseline) and a new
`run_benchmark_opt.py` (optimised). Same scorers + sample sets as the U7 suite,
so directly comparable to the qwen2.5:7b validated numbers.

## Headline

| Config | email | json | invoice | reports | speed | **composite** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **qwen2.5:7b — measured 2026-06-01** (think off, U7 prompts, ctx 8192) | 90% | 100% | 85.4% | 100% | 100% (73.7 t/s) | **96.0%** |
| qwen3.5:9b baseline (think on, plain prompts) | — | — | — | — | — | **DNF** (43s/email, then >120s timeout) |
| qwen3.5:9b optimised (think off, U7 prompts, ctx 8192) | 90% | 100% | 92.8% | 100% | 59.7% (35.8 t/s) | **83.1%** |

True head-to-head on **current hardware, identical optimised suite** (qwen2.5:7b
re-pulled and re-measured, not just the historical U7 figure):

- **Speed: qwen2.5:7b ~2× faster** — 67 t/s sustained vs 32.7, 893ms vs 1929ms
  avg email latency. qwen2.5 fits 100% on GPU (5.3GB); qwen3.5 spills 19% to CPU.
- **Composite: 96.0% vs 83.1%** — gap is entirely speed.
- Accuracy is a near-tie; qwen3.5's only win is invoice extraction (92.8 vs 85.4%).

## Optimisation levers (each independently verified)

1. **`think:false`** — biggest win. Classification dropped from ~402 eval tokens
   (with reasoning trace) to ~20 tokens. Per-email latency 43–120s → ~1.2s (~40×).
   The qwen35 model ships with `thinking` capability ON by default.
2. **U7 prompts** (`qwen_prompts.py`) over the runner's inline plain prompts —
   entity hints + enum guards. Invoice 85→93%.
3. **temp 0 / top_k 20–40** — overrides the model's baked-in `temperature=1`,
   `presence_penalty=1.5` (bad for deterministic JSON).
4. **`num_ctx` cap** — no speed effect: model still 19%/81% CPU/GPU at both 8192
   and 4096. The spill is weights+graph, not KV cache.

## Hardware ceiling

qwen3.5:9b does NOT fit the RTX 3060 (12GB): loads 12–13GB, 19% on CPU, ~32–36 t/s.
qwen2.5:7b fit fully (~70–89 t/s). Speed gap is structural to this card.

## Medium-tier comparison (all measured 2026-06-01, same suite)

| Medium candidate | invoice | reports | speed | composite |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **qwen3.5:9b** | **92.8%** | 100% | **32.7 t/s** | **83.1%** |
| phi4:14b | 91.3% | 100% | 16.6 t/s | 81.3% |

**phi4:14b is strictly dominated** — slower (½ the throughput) AND less accurate
than qwen3.5:9b. Confirms the long-standing "phi4 doesn't earn its medium tier" note.

## Recommendation / final stack

- **Hot tier: qwen2.5:7b** — composite 96.0%, 67 t/s, fits GPU. Restored 2026-06-01.
- **Medium tier: qwen3.5:9b** — beats phi4 on both accuracy and speed. Recommend
  repointing `medium` config (SPEC §router) from phi4:14b → qwen3.5:9b. NOT yet
  done — touches router config, needs a deliberate change.
- **phi4:14b** — re-pulled for config robustness, but a candidate for removal once
  medium is repointed.
- **Do NOT adopt qwen3.5:9b as the hot tier** — fails the +3% deploy threshold on
  speed (CPU-spills, ~2× slower than qwen2.5:7b).
- heavy = llama3.3:70b (older SPEC) NOT restored: 43GB, won't fit the 12GB 3060,
  and absent from today's documented inventory.

