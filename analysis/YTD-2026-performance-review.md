## Executive summary

**This is a five-month business pretending to be a twelve-month one** — and, for the first time, we
can see its *margins*. Revenue ran from **£7.9k in January to £151.5k in May (a 19× swing)**; every
pound of profit is made April–September. The product is genuinely good (TripAdvisor 5.0, Google 4.6),
accommodation rate and review scores are climbing, and in-season labour is run tightly (29% in May).

**What changed since v1:** the "£140k COGS black hole" turned out to be **mostly phantom** — £95k of
bank/card/supplier *statements* were being miscounted as cost (some double-counting real invoices). We
purged them, drained the stuck supplier line-data, and can now publish **real gross margins**:

| | Real GP | Read |
|---|---:|---|
| 🍽️ **Food** | **≈ 64%** | Healthy, on-benchmark for a gastro-led kitchen |
| 🍺 **Drink** | **≈ 53%** | **Low** — a pub should run 58–65%; *this is money on the table* |
| **Blended trade** | **≈ 60%** | Bankable for the first time |

The headline insight: **your kitchen is well-run, your bar is leaking margin.** Fix the drink GP and the
off-season roster, and a fundamentally sound business becomes a confidently profitable one.

one to interrogate — capex or repair?

## 4. Demand drivers — weather & reviews

**Reviews are a balance-sheet asset:** TripAdvisor **5.0/5** (12) · Google **4.6/5** (25) · Booking
**8.97/10** (36). That reputation is *why* ADR climbs £104→£156. **Reviews → rate → revenue** —
systematise the post-stay/post-meal ask; 73 reviews is too thin to defend the rate long-term.

**Weather moves the till — but not as folklore says:**
- **Temperature is the strongest lever** (corr +0.36) > sunshine (+0.28); rain mildly negative (−0.18).
- **Rain changes a day, doesn't kill it:** wet days 143 covers @ **£21.8/head**; dry days 224 covers @
  **£15.5/head**. Fewer bodies, ~40% more each. **Staff and merchandise *to* the forecast** — lighter
  front-of-house, push food and a second round on wet days.

## 7. Verdict & five moves

**Verdict:** A good business — strong product, real pricing power, disciplined kitchen — running at
roughly breakeven through the shoulder season with all the profit still ahead, and **one clear,
quantified margin leak at the bar.** The trading is sound; the wins now are *operational and pricing*,
not existential.

1. **Fix the drink GP.** 53% → 60% on ~£89k of wet sales is **~£6k straight to the bottom line.** Keg-by-keg
   review now that line data exists; check pour control and guest-line pricing. **Fastest cash win on the page.**
2. **Re-engineer the off-season roster.** A 57–63% winter labour line is structural; model a leaner
   Nov–Mar crew, target sub-45%.
3. **Split the £86k maintenance into capex vs. repair** — it's distorting the true trading margin.
4. **Push accommodation.** Highest margin, rising rate, review-fed; add a revenue-management nudge.
5. **Industrialise reviews & settle Trail.** Double review volume to defend the rate; reinstate the
   compliance checklists or kill the system — a 0% score nobody acts on is worse than none.
